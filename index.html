About Me as Researcher:

I'm a doctoral student at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) and a proud member of Intentional Design Studio. As an interdisciplinary researcher, my work lies in the field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), critical and cultural computing and games studies, queer/trans studies, game design, and game art. I use computational media as the research lens to explore the intersections of culture, design, and marginalized identities. I have worked as a Teaching Assistant at WPI Academic & Research Computing and Research Assistant at WPI Computer Science since February 2021.

About Me as Developer:

I'm also a 2D/3D game artist, multi-skill developer, designer, and bartender. Before embarking on a career as an HCI researcher, I completed a Bachelor's program in Video Game Art and Animation at the Hubei Institute of Fine Arts (HIFA) in Wuhan, China, in 2018. I instructed the game studio course for the HIFA School of Animation as a Teaching Assistant, while founded the game company Wuhan KBOOM Network Technology Co. LTD. These experiences motivated me to contemplate the interrelation among computational media, identity, and culture; consequently, this led to my decision to pursue a career in HCI research.
